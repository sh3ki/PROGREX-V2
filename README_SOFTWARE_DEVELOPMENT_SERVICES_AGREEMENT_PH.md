### 2. Definitions

For purposes of this Agreement:

2.1 **"Services"** means all software development and related professional services described in Schedule A (Statement of Work).  
2.2 **"Deliverables"** means all outputs to be delivered by Developer, including source code, object code, documentation, designs, and reports specified in Schedule A.  
2.3 **"Acceptance Criteria"** means measurable standards in Schedule A used to evaluate Deliverables.  
2.4 **"Change Request"** means a written request signed/approved by both Parties to alter scope, timeline, cost, or specifications.  
2.5 **"Confidential Information"** means non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other, in any form, marked or reasonably understood as confidential.  
2.6 **"Intellectual Property Rights"** means all rights to inventions, patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, database rights, and analogous rights worldwide.  
2.7 **"Applicable Law"** means all laws, regulations, rules, and governmental requirements applicable to this Agreement, including Philippine laws if selected in Clause 24.

### 8. Milestones, Delivery, and Acceptance

8.1 Milestones and delivery dates are listed in Schedule A.  
8.2 Upon delivery of a milestone Deliverable, Client shall have **[5/10/15]** business days ("Review Period") to either:
- issue written acceptance; or
- issue a written rejection with specific non-conformities to Acceptance Criteria.

8.3 If Client does not respond within the Review Period, the Deliverable is deemed accepted.  
8.4 Developer shall correct valid non-conformities within a reasonable period and re-submit for acceptance.  
8.5 Minor defects that do not materially impair intended use shall not justify rejection but must be corrected in the next patch cycle.

9.1 Any change to scope, Deliverables, technical architecture, schedule, assumptions, or fees must be documented in a Change Request.  
9.2 Each Change Request shall include at minimum:
- change description and rationale;
- impact on timeline and cost;
- dependencies and responsibilities;
- acceptance impact.

9.3 No changed work is required until Change Request approval by both Parties.

### 13. Intellectual Property and License

13.1 **Pre-Existing IP:** Each Party retains ownership of its pre-existing materials, tools, frameworks, know-how, and IP existing prior to this Agreement or developed independently outside this Agreement.

13.2 **Developed Deliverables:** Subject to full payment of all amounts due, the Developer assigns to Client all right, title, and interest in custom Deliverables specifically created for Client under this Agreement, except Developer Pre-Existing IP and Third-Party Materials.

13.3 **Developer Tools License:** To the extent Developer Pre-Existing IP is embedded in Deliverables, Developer grants Client a perpetual, non-exclusive, royalty-free, worldwide license to use, reproduce, and modify such embedded portions solely as necessary to use the Deliverables.

13.4 **Third-Party and Open Source Components:**
- Developer may use third-party/open source components only if disclosed in writing;
- Developer shall provide an OSS/third-party component list with applicable licenses;
- Developer warrants that such use will not knowingly impose undisclosed obligations inconsistent with Client's intended commercial use.

13.5 **Moral Rights:** To the extent legally permissible, Developer shall procure waivers/consents from personnel for moral rights needed for Client's intended use.

13.6 **Further Assurances:** Each Party agrees to execute documents reasonably necessary to effectuate IP provisions.

### 14. Confidentiality

14.1 Receiving Party shall:
- use Confidential Information only for performing this Agreement;
- restrict disclosure to personnel/contractors with need-to-know and equivalent confidentiality obligations;
- protect Confidential Information using at least reasonable care.

14.2 Exclusions: information that is publicly available without breach, already lawfully known, independently developed, or lawfully received from third parties without confidentiality duty.

14.3 If disclosure is required by law or court order, Receiving Party shall (where legally allowed) provide prompt notice to enable protective action.

14.4 Confidentiality obligations survive for **[3/5]** years after termination, and indefinitely for trade secrets to the extent permitted by law.

### 15. Data Privacy and Security (Philippines)

15.1 Where personal data is processed, Parties shall comply with Republic Act No. 10173 (Data Privacy Act of 2012), its IRR, and applicable National Privacy Commission issuances.

15.2 Roles (select and define):
- Client as Personal Information Controller (PIC), Developer as Personal Information Processor (PIP); or
- other arrangement as documented in Schedule C (Data Processing Terms).

15.3 Developer shall implement organizational, physical, and technical safeguards appropriate to risk and data sensitivity.

15.4 Developer shall notify Client without undue delay, and in any event within **[24/48/72]** hours, after becoming aware of a confirmed or reasonably suspected personal data breach affecting Client data.

15.5 Cross-border data transfers, if any, shall comply with applicable law and Client instructions.

### 16. Warranties

16.1 Mutual Authority Warranty: Each Party warrants it has full power and authority to enter this Agreement.

16.2 Developer Performance Warranty: For **[30/60/90]** days from acceptance of each Deliverable, Developer warrants that Deliverables will substantially conform to Acceptance Criteria.

16.3 Remedy: For valid warranty claims timely notified by Client, Developer shall, at its expense, re-perform/correct non-conforming Deliverables. If Developer fails to cure within a reasonable time, Client may seek proportionate refund for affected Deliverables.

16.4 Exclusions: Warranty does not cover defects caused by Client modifications, misuse, third-party systems not controlled by Developer, or use contrary to documentation.

16.5 Except as expressly stated, Deliverables are provided "as is" and all implied warranties are disclaimed to the extent permitted by law.

---

### 17. Indemnification

17.1 Developer shall defend, indemnify, and hold Client harmless from third-party claims that Deliverables (excluding Client materials and unauthorized modifications) infringe Intellectual Property Rights, subject to:
- prompt written notice by Client;
- Developer control of defense/settlement (no admission by Client without consent);
- Client reasonable cooperation.

17.2 If infringement is likely, Developer may, at its option and expense: (a) secure right for continued use, (b) modify/replace Deliverables to be non-infringing without material loss of function, or (c) if not commercially reasonable, terminate affected Services and refund unused/prepaid fees for affected portion.

17.3 Client shall indemnify Developer against third-party claims arising from Client-provided materials, data, or instructions that infringe rights or violate law.

### 18. Limitation of Liability

18.1 Neither Party is liable for indirect, incidental, special, exemplary, or consequential damages, including loss of profits/revenue/data, even if advised of possibility.

18.2 Aggregate liability of each Party under this Agreement shall not exceed total fees paid/payable under this Agreement in the **[12]** months preceding the event giving rise to liability.

18.3 Exclusions from cap/limitations (to extent allowed by law):
- fraud or willful misconduct;
- unpaid fees;
- breach of confidentiality;
- infringement indemnity obligations;
- data privacy violations caused by gross negligence or willful misconduct.

---

### 19. Independent Contractor Relationship

19.1 Developer is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of Client.  
19.2 Developer has sole control over means and methods of work, subject to contractual specifications.  
19.3 Developer is responsible for salaries, benefits, taxes, and statutory obligations of its personnel.  
19.4 No employer-employee relationship is created between Client and Developer personnel.

---

### 20. Suspension and Termination

20.1 Either Party may terminate for cause upon material breach by the other Party not cured within **[15/30]** days from written notice.

20.2 Either Party may terminate immediately if the other Party:
- becomes insolvent, ceases operations, or enters liquidation/rehabilitation;
- commits fraud, gross misconduct, or unlawful acts materially affecting this Agreement.

20.3 Convenience Termination (optional): Client may terminate without cause upon **[30]** days written notice, provided Client pays for Services properly performed and non-cancellable commitments incurred before effectivity.

20.4 Upon termination:
- Client shall pay all undisputed amounts due for accepted work and approved work-in-progress;
- Developer shall deliver completed/paid Deliverables and return or securely delete Client Confidential Information as instructed;
- each Party shall return property of the other, subject to legal retention obligations.

20.5 Clauses that by nature should survive (including confidentiality, IP, limitations, indemnities, dispute terms, unpaid fees) shall survive termination.

24.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Republic of the Philippines.

24.2 The Parties shall first attempt in good faith to resolve disputes through executive-level negotiation within **[15/30]** days from written dispute notice.

24.3 If unresolved, the dispute shall be submitted to mediation in **[CITY], Philippines** under mutually agreed mediation rules.

24.4 If still unresolved, choose one option and delete the other:
- **Arbitration Option:** Final and binding arbitration in **[CITY], Philippines** under the ADR framework and agreed institutional/ad hoc rules. Language: English. Number of arbitrators: **[1/3]**.
- **Court Option:** Exclusive venue/jurisdiction of proper courts in **[CITY], Philippines**.

24.5 Nothing prevents either Party from seeking urgent injunctive relief from competent courts to prevent irreparable harm.
